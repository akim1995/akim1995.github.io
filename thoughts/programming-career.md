# This Time, the Layer That Moved Is the Code

Assemblers were faster at writing binary than humans. Compilers were faster at writing assembly. AI is faster at writing code than programmers. We, programmers, have been through this before. But none of those leaps went after the code itself.

Each time, the layer moves up. Each time, the work we knew became the floor the next thing stood on. We spent years learning what the machine was doing and why. Then everything we put into our heads got put into a model. The knowledge did not disappear. It just stopped being scarce. That is a strange thing to sit with.

Every transition splits the room — those who find a technical objection and wait behind it, and those who move before the ground is solid. Right now the objection is non-determinism. The developer you hired last month was also non-deterministic. So is evolution — the most reliable process we know runs entirely on random mutation and selection pressure. Randomness with the right constraints does not produce chaos. It produces something that works. The constraint is the work.

And that work is architectural. Pick the wrong framework and the agent will tangle itself after a few iterations and not recover. Functional or not? Typed? How do you split modules so the AI can reason about them? What tools do you give it — can it check its own output, run against what it just wrote? Every decision before the agent touches the code decides whether anything useful comes out the other side. Get it wrong and you will know fast. Get it right and you will build things you could not have built before.

This is where it separates. The developers most at risk are the ones who knew the machine deeply and stayed there — that knowledge got commodified. Watching that happen is not easy. The ones who had both the technical and the human side have a foundation — but foundation is not enough. The extra step is learning the agent itself: what it needs, how to shape it, how to give it the right conditions.

The ground is still shifting. Every generation of developers had to find a new floor. We are finding ours. That is scary.
